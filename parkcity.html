Welcome to Park City

Located in the scenic Wasatch Mountains, Park City is Utah's premier ski destination and a hub for sports enthusiasts and film lovers alike. Known for hosting the famous Sundance Film Festival and boasting luxurious resorts, Park City offers an unparalleled mountain experience. In winter, its ski slopes attract visitors from around the globe, while in summer, the hills transform into ideal trails for hiking and mountain biking

City Facts

  • Population: Park City has a population of about 8,500 residents. Despite its relatively small size, the city’s fame as a world-class ski destination attracts millions of visitors annually, swelling its numbers during peak tourist seasons, especially in winter.

  • Incorporated: Park City was incorporated in 1884, following its establishment as a silver mining town in the mid-19th century. Today, while the mines have closed, Park City thrives as a resort town known for its luxury amenities and outdoor activities year-round.

  • Region: Located in northern Utah, just 30 miles east of Salt Lake City, Park City is nestled in the Wasatch Range. The area is famed for its ski resorts, including Park City Mountain and Deer Valley, which offer world-class slopes and facilities.

  • Classification: Park City is classified as a resort town, with a vibrant downtown that includes upscale shops, gourmet restaurants, and a thriving arts scene. The city is also home to the prestigious Sundance Film Festival, attracting celebrities and film enthusiasts from around the world each year.

  • Average Income: The average household income in Park City is approximately $110,000, reflecting the affluence brought by its luxury tourism and resort industries. While the cost of living is high, the city’s thriving economy offers numerous opportunities in tourism, real estate, and hospitality.

Welcome to Moab

Nestled in southeastern Utah, Moab is a hidden gem for nature lovers and adventurers. Surrounded by the breathtaking landscapes of Arches and Canyonlands National Parks, this small yet vibrant town offers access to some of the world’s most iconic rock formations. With its growing tourism scene, Moab has become renowned for its opportunities in hiking, mountain biking, and rafting along the Colorado River, making it a paradise for those looking to immerse themselves in the beauty of the desert.

City Facts

  • Population: Moab is home to approximately 5,500 residents. Despite its small size, Moab attracts thousands of tourists annually, especially during peak seasons, due to its proximity to major national parks and its reputation as an outdoor adventure hub.

  • Incorporated: Moab was incorporated as a city in 1902, although its history stretches back much further, with indigenous peoples inhabiting the area for thousands of years. The town became an important stop for travelers and later, a hotspot for uranium mining in the 20th century before transforming into an outdoor recreation destination.

  • Region: Moab is located in southeastern Utah, within the high desert region known for its stunning red rock formations. The town is a gateway to Arches and Canyonlands National Parks, offering breathtaking natural beauty and outdoor activities like hiking, mountain biking, and off-roading.

  • Classification: Moab is classified as a small rural city, but its tourism industry has earned it international recognition. Despite its remote location, Moab has a vibrant downtown filled with local shops, restaurants, and services catering to adventurers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Average Income: The average household income in Moab is around $60,000. The economy of Moab relies heavily on tourism, with many residents working in hospitality, retail, and outdoor recreation industries. While the cost of living can be higher than in other rural areas due to its popularity, the scenic beauty and outdoor lifestyle make it a desirable place to live for those passionate about nature.
